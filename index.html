Features

Lightning

Various sorts of light implemented; Ambient, Point Light, Directional & Spotlight

BRDFs

Support for different BRDFs, to enable complex shading techniques and light interactions

Camera

The camera is a Pinhole camera implementation, with an infinite view distance. Uses various parameters to orient the camera to define the view

Materials

Materials include simple implementations such as Cosine and Matte, along with complex Phong and Reflective surfaces

Ray Tracers

Two ray tracers are supported; the basic tracer which shoots primary rays into the scene, and the Whitted tracer that shoots secondary rays to support reflective materials (up to a certain depth)

Geometry

Different primitives include Sphere, Triangle, Rectangle and Plane. Each primitive has a different interaction defined with the light and the incoming rays

Compilation Instructions


For the compilation command to work, the pwd must be the raytracer folder inside the repo root.
Compilation command: g++ raytracer.cpp cameras/*.cpp geometry/*.cpp lights/*.cpp materials/*.cpp samplers/*.cpp tracers/*.cpp utilities/*.cpp world/*.cpp BRDF/*.cpp acceleration/*.cpp build/buildObj.cpp build/buildHelloWorld.cpp -Wall
Acceleration can be turned on by specifying the accel argument when calling the executable. Use ./a.out accel or an otherwise command to accelerate the structure. To try out various buildfiles, change the last build/.cpp to your preference.
